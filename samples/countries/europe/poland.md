## History

Poland was a major European power in the medieval and early modern periods. It was partitioned and disappeared from the map (1795-1918). Reborn after WWI, it was devastated by WWII and Nazi occupation, including the Holocaust. Communist rule ended in 1989.

Poland joined NATO in 1999 and the EU in 2004.

## Geography

Poland is mostly lowland with mountains (Carpathians, Sudetes) in the south. The Baltic Sea provides coastline. Major rivers include the Vistula and Oder.

## Economy

Poland has the sixth-largest EU economy, with strong growth since 1990. Manufacturing, services, and agriculture are key sectors. The country has not adopted the euro.
