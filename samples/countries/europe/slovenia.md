## History

Slovenia was part of the Habsburg Empire and then Yugoslavia. It declared independence in 1991, with the Ten-Day War resulting in quick success. Economic and political transition was smoother than elsewhere in former Yugoslavia.

Slovenia joined the EU in 2004 and was the first Eastern European country to hold the EU presidency (2008).

## Geography

Slovenia has diverse landscapes: Julian Alps, Karst region (limestone caves), and a small Adriatic coast. Lake Bled is a famous destination. The climate varies from Alpine to Mediterranean.

## Economy

Slovenia has the highest GDP per capita of former socialist countries. Manufacturing, services, and tourism are key sectors. The country has a well-educated workforce.

## Culture

Slovenian culture is Central European. The country is known for outdoor activities, wine regions, and the charming capital Ljubljana. Beekeeping (especially Carniolan bees) is a tradition.
