## History

Bosnia was part of the Ottoman Empire for centuries before Austro-Hungarian annexation. It became part of Yugoslavia after WWI. The Bosnian War (1992-1995) killed over 100,000 people, including the Srebrenica genocide.

The Dayton Agreement ended the war, creating a complex state with two entities: the Federation and Republika Srpska.

## Geography

Bosnia is mostly mountainous, with the Dinaric Alps dominating. The country has a tiny Adriatic coastline. Rivers include the Neretva and Una. The climate is continental.

## Economy

Bosnia has a developing economy still recovering from war. Manufacturing, mining, and services are key sectors. The Dayton structure creates governance inefficiencies. EU candidate status is a goal.

## Culture

Bosnian culture reflects its Bosniak, Croat, and Serb populations. Sarajevo is known for its historical multi-ethnic character and Ottoman heritage. Sevdah music is a distinctive tradition.
