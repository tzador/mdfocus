## History

The Kingdom of Hungary was a major medieval power before Ottoman and Habsburg rule. After WWI, Hungary lost two-thirds of its territory. Communist rule followed WWII until the 1989 transition.

Hungary joined NATO in 1999 and the EU in 2004. Viktor OrbÃ¡n has governed since 2010 with increasing authoritarianism.

## Geography

Hungary is mostly flat, with the Great Plain covering the east. The Danube and Tisza rivers are major waterways. Lake Balaton is Central Europe's largest lake.

## Economy

Hungary has a developed economy with strong automotive and electronics manufacturing. Budapest is a financial center. EU tensions have affected some funding.

## Culture

Hungarian culture is distinctive, with a unique Uralic language. Cuisine (goulash, paprika), music (Liszt), and thermal baths are famous. Budapest's architecture spans Roman to Art Nouveau.
