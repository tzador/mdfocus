**Andorra**, officially the **Principality of Andorra**, is a tiny landlocked country in the Pyrenees mountains between France and Spain. Known for ski resorts and duty-free shopping, it is a parliamentary co-principality.

## History

Andorra dates to Charlemagne's era, with a charter from 988. The unique co-principality, with French and Spanish (Episcopal) co-princes, has existed since 1278. The country modernized significantly after WWII.

Andorra democratized in 1993 with a new constitution while retaining its co-princes.

## Geography

Andorra is entirely mountainous, with 65 peaks over 2,000 meters. The Valira River drains the country. The climate is alpine, with heavy snowfall supporting ski tourism.

## Economy

Tourism (skiing, shopping) and banking dominate the economy. There is no income tax. The country uses the euro but is not an EU member. Retail attracts visitors seeking duty-free goods.

## Culture

Andorran culture is Catalan-influenced. Romanesque churches dot the valleys. The country celebrates traditional festivals and has a parliament (General Council) dating to the 13th century.
