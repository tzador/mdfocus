## History

Belgium was part of various European powers before gaining independence from the Netherlands in 1830. It was occupied in both World Wars. Belgian Congo was one of the most brutal colonial regimes.

Belgium was a founding member of the EU and hosts major European institutions in Brussels.

## Geography

Belgium is mostly flat, with the Ardennes hills in the southeast. The North Sea coast is short but developed. Major rivers include the Meuse and Scheldt.

## Economy

Belgium has a highly developed, diversified economy. Brussels hosts EU institutions and international organizations. Port of Antwerp is Europe's second-largest. Pharmaceuticals, chemicals, and manufacturing are key sectors.

## Culture

Belgium is divided between Dutch-speaking Flanders and French-speaking Wallonia. The country is famous for chocolate, beer, waffles, and comics (Tintin, the Smurfs). Medieval towns like Bruges attract tourists.
