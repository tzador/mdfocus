**Vatican City**, officially the **Vatican City State**, is the world's smallest independent state. The spiritual and administrative center of the Roman Catholic Church, it is entirely within Rome.

## History

Vatican City was created by the Lateran Treaty with Italy in 1929, resolving the "Roman Question" after Italian unification absorbed the Papal States. The Pope has been the spiritual head of Catholicism for nearly 2,000 years.

## Geography

Vatican City is entirely within Rome, enclosed by walls. It includes St. Peter's Basilica, the Sistine Chapel, Vatican Museums, and papal apartments.

## Economy

Vatican finances come from donations (Peter's Pence), museum admissions, and publishing. The Vatican Bank manages church finances.

## Culture

Vatican City houses some of humanity's greatest art treasures, including Michelangelo's Sistine Chapel ceiling and St. Peter's Basilica. Swiss Guards protect the Pope. The Vatican has its own media and postal service.
