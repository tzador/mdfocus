## History

Denmark was the center of a Viking empire and medieval Nordic power. It lost territory to Sweden and Germany over centuries. Constitutional monarchy emerged in 1849. Denmark was occupied by Nazi Germany in WWII.

Denmark joined the EU but maintains opt-outs, including keeping its currency.

## Geography

Denmark consists of the Jutland Peninsula and numerous islands, including Zealand (home to Copenhagen). The terrain is flat and agricultural. The climate is temperate maritime.

## Economy

Denmark has a highly developed mixed economy with strong social welfare. Pharmaceuticals (Novo Nordisk), shipping (Maersk), and wind energy are key sectors. It has the highest income equality in the EU.

## Culture

Danish culture values "hygge" (coziness) and design. The country has produced Hans Christian Andersen and Kierkegaard. Danish modern design and architecture are globally influential.
