## History

Italy was the center of the Roman Empire and the Renaissance. The country unified in 1861 after centuries of division. Fascism under Mussolini ended with WWII defeat.

Italy was a founding member of the EU and NATO. It has faced political instability with frequent government changes.

## Geography

Italy is a boot-shaped peninsula with the Alps in the north and Mediterranean islands (Sicily, Sardinia). The Apennine Mountains run the length of the country. Volcanic activity (Vesuvius, Etna) is significant.

## Economy

Italy has the world's eighth-largest economy, with manufacturing (fashion, automotive, machinery), tourism, and agriculture as key sectors. The north-south economic divide persists.

## Culture

Italian culture has shaped Western civilization through art, architecture, music (opera), and cuisine. The country has more UNESCO World Heritage Sites than any other. Fashion houses (Gucci, Prada) and Ferrari are global icons.
