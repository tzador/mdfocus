## History

Portugal pioneered the Age of Discovery in the 15th-16th centuries, establishing a vast empire. A long dictatorship (1933-1974) ended with the Carnation Revolution. Colonial wars ended, and democracy was established.

Portugal joined the EU in 1986 and the Eurozone in 1999. The 2011 debt crisis required an international bailout.

## Geography

Portugal occupies the western Iberian coast with the Atlantic. The Azores and Madeira are Atlantic archipelagos. The terrain includes mountains, plains, and extensive coastline.

## Economy

Portugal has a developed economy with tourism, manufacturing, and services. The country has attracted tech investment and digital nomads. Port wine is a famous export.

## Culture

Portuguese culture is distinctive, with fado music, historic architecture, and traditions of the maritime age. Portuguese is spoken by over 250 million people globally due to the colonial legacy.
