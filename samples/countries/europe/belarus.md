## History

Belarus was part of the Grand Duchy of Lithuania and then the Russian Empire. It briefly gained independence before becoming a Soviet republic. Independence came in 1991.

Lukashenko has ruled since 1994. A disputed 2020 election sparked mass protests that were brutally suppressed. Belarus assisted Russia's 2022 invasion of Ukraine.

## Geography

Belarus is mostly flat, with forests and wetlands. The Pripyat Marshes are Europe's largest wetland. The country was heavily affected by the Chernobyl disaster.

## Economy

Belarus has a state-controlled economy with Soviet-era industries. Potash and oil refining are important exports. Western sanctions have increased economic dependence on Russia.

## Culture

Belarusian culture reflects its position between Poland and Russia. Folk traditions, Orthodox Christianity, and Soviet legacy shape identity. Traditional crafts include straw weaving.
