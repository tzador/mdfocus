**Botswana**, officially the **Republic of Botswana**, is a landlocked country in Southern Africa. Known for its remarkable post-independence economic growth and stable democracy, it has transformed from one of the world's poorest nations to an upper-middle-income country.

## History

The area of modern Botswana was inhabited by San people for thousands of years before Bantu-speaking tribes migrated to the region. In the 19th century, the territory came under British protection as Bechuanaland Protectorate to prevent Boer expansion. Botswana gained independence peacefully on September 30, 1966.

Under the leadership of Seretse Khama, the country discovered diamonds and wisely invested the revenue in infrastructure, education, and healthcare, becoming one of Africa's greatest success stories.

## Geography

The Kalahari Desert covers approximately 70% of Botswana's territory. Despite its arid nature, the country is home to the Okavango Delta, one of the world's largest inland deltas and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The delta supports incredible wildlife diversity and is a premier safari destination.

## Economy

Botswana has one of the world's fastest-growing economies since independence, largely due to diamond mining. The country is the world's largest producer of diamonds by value. The government has maintained prudent fiscal policies and invested heavily in education and infrastructure. Efforts to diversify include tourism and financial services.

## Culture

Botswana's culture is centered on Tswana traditions, with a strong emphasis on community and respect for elders. The country is known for its traditional music, basket weaving, and cattle culture. The annual President's Day celebrations showcase traditional dance, music, and crafts.
