## History

Somalia has ancient trade connections, part of the Land of Punt mentioned by ancient Egyptians. Arab influence brought Islam. Italy and Britain colonized different regions in the 19th century. Unified Somalia gained independence on July 1, 1960.

Siad Barre's dictatorship (1969-1991) was followed by civil war and state collapse. Warlordism, Al-Shabaab insurgency, and piracy have plagued the country. Federal government was re-established in 2012.

## Geography

Somalia occupies the Horn of Africa, with the Gulf of Aden to the north and Indian Ocean to the east. The terrain includes coastal plains, plateaus, and highlands. The climate is hot year-round, with irregular rainfall causing frequent droughts. Somaliland in the northwest operates as an unrecognized independent state.

## Economy

Somalia has a predominantly pastoral economy, with livestock being the main export. Remittances from the diaspora are crucial. Despite conflict, informal sectors and telecommunications have developed. Piracy off the coast has declined from its peak.

## Culture

Somali culture is characterized by clan identity, oral poetry, and Islamic tradition. Poetry is highly valued and poets historically served as spokespersons. Nomadic pastoralism has shaped customs. Somali cuisine features camel meat, rice, and xalwo (a sweet confection).
