## History

The territory was home to various kingdoms and chiefdoms before German colonization in 1884. After World War I, the colony was divided between France and Britain. French Cameroon gained independence in 1960, and British Cameroons joined in 1961 to form the Federal Republic of Cameroon.

Paul Biya has been president since 1982, making him one of the world's longest-serving leaders. The country has faced ongoing conflict in the Anglophone regions since 2016.

## Geography

Cameroon features diverse landscapes: dense rainforests in the south, semi-arid plains in the north, and the volcanic Mount Cameroon (4,095 m), the highest peak in West-Central Africa. The country has coastline along the Bight of Biafra and shares borders with six countries.

## Economy

Cameroon has one of the most diversified economies in sub-Saharan Africa, with oil, timber, cocoa, and coffee as major exports. Agriculture employs about 70% of the workforce. The country serves as an economic hub for the Central African region and hosts the headquarters of several regional organizations.

## Culture

Cameroon is one of Africa's most culturally diverse nations, with over 250 ethnic groups and languages. It's renowned for its football heritage, producing world-class players like Roger Milla, Samuel Eto'o, and Rigobert Song. The country's music scene includes makossa and bikutsi styles.
