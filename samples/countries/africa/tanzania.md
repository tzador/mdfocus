## History

Tanzania has archaeological importance, with early human fossils at Olduvai Gorge. The Swahili coast was a trading hub for centuries. Germany colonized Tanganyika, followed by British rule after WWI. Tanganyika gained independence in 1961 and united with Zanzibar in 1964.

Julius Nyerere, the first president, promoted African socialism (Ujamaa) and Swahili as a unifying language. Tanzania has remained peaceful despite regional conflicts.

## Geography

Tanzania has extraordinary geographical diversity: Mount Kilimanjaro (5,895 m, Africa's highest peak), the Serengeti plains, the Great Rift Valley with Lakes Tanganyika, Victoria, and Malawi, and the Indian Ocean coast. The Ngorongoro Crater is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

## Economy

Tanzania has experienced steady economic growth. Agriculture, particularly coffee, tea, and cashews, employs most of the population. Tourism is a major earner, alongside gold and gemstone mining. Dar es Salaam is a growing commercial hub.

## Culture

Tanzania is known for Swahili culture and language, which originated on its coast. The country has over 120 ethnic groups living in relative harmony. Taarab music blends African and Arab influences. Tingatinga paintings are internationally recognized.
