## History

The region was home to the Shona people and the medieval Kingdom of Zimbabwe, whose Great Zimbabwe ruins are a UNESCO World Heritage Site. British colonization established Southern Rhodesia. Ian Smith's white minority government declared independence unilaterally in 1965.

After a liberation war, Zimbabwe gained recognized independence in 1980 under Robert Mugabe. His 37-year rule ended in 2017 through military intervention. Emmerson Mnangagwa has governed since.

## Geography

Zimbabwe is a high plateau country with the Eastern Highlands rising to 2,592 meters. Victoria Falls forms part of the northern border with Zambia. Hwange National Park is one of Africa's largest wildlife reserves. The climate is subtropical with a rainy season.

## Economy

Zimbabwe experienced economic collapse and hyperinflation in the 2000s following controversial land reforms. Agriculture, mining (gold, platinum, diamonds), and tourism are key sectors. The economy has stabilized somewhat but remains fragile with high informal employment.

## Culture

Shona and Ndebele cultures predominate. Zimbabwe is famous for mbira music and Shona sculpture. Traditional dance and oral traditions remain important. The country has produced notable authors, including Nobel laureate Doris Lessing (who grew up there).
