**Angola**, officially the **Republic of Angola**, is a country on the west coast of Southern Africa. It is the second-largest Lusophone (Portuguese-speaking) country in both total area and population and is the seventh-largest country in Africa.

## History

The territory of present-day Angola was inhabited by various Bantu peoples before Portuguese colonization began in the 15th century. Angola was a major source of enslaved people sent to the Americas, particularly Brazil. After centuries of colonial rule, Angola gained independence from Portugal on November 11, 1975.

Independence was followed by a devastating civil war that lasted from 1975 to 2002, involving various factions supported by Cold War powers. The war ended with the death of UNITA leader Jonas Savimbi, leading to a peace agreement.

## Geography

Angola features diverse landscapes, from tropical rainforests in the north to arid deserts in the south. The country has a 1,600-kilometer Atlantic coastline and is home to the Congo Basin rainforest and the Namib Desert. The Kwanza River is the largest river flowing entirely within Angola.

## Economy

Angola is one of Africa's major oil producers and one of the world's largest diamond producers. Oil accounts for over 90% of exports and nearly 70% of government revenue. The country is working to diversify its economy through agriculture, fisheries, and manufacturing to reduce dependence on oil.

## Culture

Angolan culture reflects its Bantu heritage and Portuguese colonial history. The country is known for semba and kizomba music, which have gained international popularity.
