**Eswatini**, officially the **Kingdom of Eswatini** (formerly Swaziland), is a small landlocked country in Southern Africa. It is one of the world's last absolute monarchies and is almost entirely surrounded by South Africa.

## History

The Swazi people settled in the area in the 18th century, establishing a kingdom under the Dlamini dynasty. The country became a British protectorate in 1903 and gained independence on September 6, 1968, under King Sobhuza II.

Sobhuza II ruled for over 60 years until his death in 1982. The current king, Mswati III, ascended in 1986 and has maintained absolute power despite pro-democracy movements. The country changed its name from Swaziland to Eswatini in 2018.

## Geography

Eswatini is one of Africa's smallest countries, featuring four distinct geographical regions: the mountainous Highveld in the west, the Middleveld, the Lowveld, and the Lubombo escarpment in the east. Despite its small size, the country has notable biodiversity and several wildlife reserves.

## Economy

Eswatini has a developing economy based on agriculture, manufacturing, and services. Sugar is the main agricultural export, and the country also produces soft drink concentrates, textiles, and wood pulp. Many Swazis work in South African mines. Tourism is growing, particularly cultural tourism.

## Culture

Swazi culture is centered on the monarchy and traditional ceremonies. The Incwala (kingship ceremony) and Umhlanga (reed dance) are major annual events. Traditional crafts include basket weaving and pottery. The country has one of the world's highest HIV/AIDS prevalence rates, significantly impacting society.
