## History

Mali was the heartland of several great West African empires: Ghana (4th-13th centuries), Mali (13th-16th centuries), and Songhai (15th-16th centuries). Timbuktu became a legendary center of learning and trade. France colonized the territory in the late 19th century.

Mali gained independence in 1960. After decades of one-party rule, democracy emerged in 1991. A 2012 Tuareg rebellion and Islamist insurgency led to a French military intervention. Military coups in 2020 and 2021 have resulted in ongoing political transition.

## Geography

Mali is Africa's eighth-largest country, mostly covered by the Sahara Desert and Sahel savanna. The Niger River, the country's lifeblood, creates a fertile inland delta crucial for fishing and agriculture. Only the southern region receives adequate rainfall for farming.

## Economy

Mali is one of the world's poorest countries, with most people depending on subsistence agriculture. The country is a significant gold producer and cotton exporter. Economic development is hampered by conflict, limited infrastructure, and landlocked geography.
