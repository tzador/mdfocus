**The Republic of the Congo**, also known as **Congo-Brazzaville**, is a country in Central Africa. Not to be confused with its larger neighbor, the Democratic Republic of the Congo, it is located along the western bank of the Congo River.

## History

The region was home to several Bantu kingdoms before French colonization in the 1880s. It became part of French Equatorial Africa and served as the capital of Free France during World War II. The country gained independence on August 15, 1960.

The Congo experienced a Marxist-Leninist period (1969-1991) and a civil war in the late 1990s. Denis Sassou Nguesso has been president for most of the period since 1979.

## Geography

The Congo features coastal plains, a central plateau, and the northern Congo Basin with dense rainforests. The country has significant biodiversity and is home to populations of western lowland gorillas and forest elephants. The Congo River forms part of the eastern border with the DRC.

## Economy

The Republic of the Congo is a major oil producer, with petroleum accounting for about 90% of export revenues. Despite oil wealth, poverty remains widespread due to unequal distribution. The country is working to diversify into agriculture, forestry, and tourism. Pointe-Noire is the economic capital and main port.

## Culture

Congolese culture reflects the traditions of various ethnic groups, including the Kongo, Teke, and Mbochi peoples. The country has produced notable literary figures and musicians. Brazzaville was a crucible for Congolese rumba alongside Kinshasa, producing legendary artists.
