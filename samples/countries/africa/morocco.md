## History

Morocco has been inhabited since Paleolithic times and was home to Berber kingdoms before Arab conquest in the 7th century. Powerful dynasties, including the Almoravids, Almohads, and Saadians, built impressive imperial cities. Morocco was a French and Spanish protectorate from 1912 to 1956.

King Mohammed VI has ruled since 1999, implementing gradual reforms. Morocco claims Western Sahara, a disputed territory, creating ongoing international tensions.

## Geography

Morocco has diverse geography: the Rif and Atlas mountain ranges, Atlantic and Mediterranean coasts, fertile plains, and the Sahara Desert in the south. The highest peak is Jebel Toubkal (4,167 m). The country has a varied climate, from Mediterranean to semi-arid.

## Economy

Morocco has a diversified economy including agriculture (world's largest phosphate exporter), manufacturing, tourism, and services. The country has invested heavily in renewable energy, particularly solar. The automotive and aerospace industries are growing. Casablanca is the economic capital.

## Culture

Moroccan culture blends Berber, Arab, and French influences. The country is famous for its imperial cities (Marrakech, Fez, Meknes, Rabat), intricate craftsmanship, and cuisine featuring tagines and couscous. Music ranges from Gnawa spiritual traditions to contemporary chaabi pop.
