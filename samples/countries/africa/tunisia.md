**Tunisia**, officially the **Republic of Tunisia**, is the northernmost country in Africa. The birthplace of the Arab Spring in 2011, it is known for its Mediterranean coastline, Carthaginian ruins, and blend of Arab and French influences.

## History

Tunisia was the center of ancient Carthage, Rome's great rival. The region was later ruled by various Muslim dynasties and the Ottoman Empire. France established a protectorate in 1881. Independence came on March 20, 1956.

Habib Bourguiba modernized the country until his 1987 ouster. Zine El Abidine Ben Ali ruled until the 2011 Jasmine Revolution, which sparked the Arab Spring across the region. Democratic progress has faced setbacks since 2021.

## Geography

Tunisia has a Mediterranean coast in the north, the Atlas Mountains, and the Sahara Desert in the south. The climate is Mediterranean in the north, becoming arid southward. The country is relatively small but geographically diverse.

## Economy

Tunisia has a diversified economy including manufacturing, tourism, agriculture, and services. It is a major olive oil exporter. The country has stronger economic ties with Europe than most African nations. However, unemployment, particularly among youth, remains high.

## Culture

Tunisian culture blends Berber, Arab, and French influences. The medina of Tunis and the ruins of Carthage are UNESCO sites. The country is known for harissa (hot pepper paste) and couscous cuisine. Tunisian oud music and Sufi traditions are distinctive.
