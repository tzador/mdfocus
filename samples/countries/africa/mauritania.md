**Mauritania**, officially the **Islamic Republic of Mauritania**, is a country in Northwest Africa. Largely covered by the Sahara Desert, it serves as a bridge between Arab North Africa and sub-Saharan Africa.

## History

Mauritania was part of the ancient Ghana and Almoravid empires. French colonization began in the late 19th century. Independence came on November 28, 1960. The country has experienced multiple coups, with the most recent transition to elected government in 2019.

Mauritania was the last country to officially abolish slavery in 1981, though forms of servitude persist. The country faces ongoing challenges with discrimination and inequality.

## Geography

About 90% of Mauritania lies within the Sahara Desert, making it one of the world's least densely populated countries. The Senegal River in the south provides the only significant arable land. The Banc d'Arguin National Park on the coast is a major bird sanctuary.

## Economy

Mauritania's economy depends on iron ore mining, fishing, and livestock herding. The country has significant offshore oil potential. Most of the population practices nomadic or semi-nomadic pastoralism. Nouakchott has grown rapidly from a small village to a city of over one million.

## Culture

Mauritanian culture blends Arab-Berber and sub-Saharan African influences. The country is known for its distinctive Moorish music and poetry. Traditional hospitality involves elaborate tea ceremonies. The ancient libraries of Chinguetti contain thousands of medieval manuscripts.
