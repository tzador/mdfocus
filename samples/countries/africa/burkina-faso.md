**Burkina Faso**, meaning "Land of Incorruptible People," is a landlocked country in West Africa. Formerly known as Upper Volta during French colonial rule, the country was renamed in 1984 by President Thomas Sankara.

## History

The region was home to several powerful kingdoms, including the Mossi kingdoms, which resisted Islamic conquest for centuries. France colonized the area in the late 19th century, establishing French Upper Volta. Independence came on August 5, 1960.

Thomas Sankara, who led a revolution in 1983, implemented progressive social programs and is often called "Africa's Che Guevara." He was assassinated in 1987. The country has experienced political instability, including multiple coups in recent years.

## Geography

Burkina Faso is characterized by savanna landscapes, with the Sahel region in the north and wooded savanna in the south. The country has three main rivers: the Black Volta, White Volta, and Red Volta. The climate is tropical with distinct wet and dry seasons.

## Economy

Burkina Faso is one of the world's poorest countries, with agriculture employing about 80% of the population. Cotton is the main export crop, and gold mining has become increasingly important. The country also produces mangoes, sesame, and shea nuts.
