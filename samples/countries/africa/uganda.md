**Uganda**, officially the **Republic of Uganda**, is a landlocked country in East Africa. Known as the "Pearl of Africa" for its natural beauty, it is home to Lake Victoria's source of the Nile and mountain gorillas.

## History

The region had organized kingdoms, including Buganda, before British colonization in the late 19th century. Uganda gained independence on October 9, 1962. The brutal rule of Idi Amin (1971-1979) killed hundreds of thousands.

Yoweri Museveni has been president since 1986, bringing stability but facing criticism for authoritarian tendencies. The country has dealt with insurgencies, including Joseph Kony's Lord's Resistance Army.

## Geography

Uganda straddles the equator with a temperate climate due to elevation. The country shares Lake Victoria, Africa's largest lake, and has other significant lakes and rivers. The Rwenzori Mountains ("Mountains of the Moon") rise to over 5,000 meters. Bwindi Impenetrable Forest hosts mountain gorillas.

## Economy

Uganda's economy is based on agriculture, with coffee being the main export. The country has discovered oil in the Albertine Graben region. Tourism, particularly gorilla trekking, is growing. Economic growth has been strong, though inequality persists.

## Culture

Uganda has diverse ethnic groups, including the Baganda, Banyankole, and Acholi. Traditional kingdoms, particularly Buganda, remain culturally significant. Music ranges from traditional drums to Afrobeat and kidandali. The country has produced notable athletes, particularly in distance running.
