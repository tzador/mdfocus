## History

Kenya has a rich archaeological heritage, with fossils suggesting it may be the cradle of humankind. The Swahili coast was part of Indian Ocean trade networks for centuries. Britain colonized the interior in the late 19th century, establishing the Kenya Colony.

The Mau Mau Uprising (1952-1960) was a significant anti-colonial movement. Kenya gained independence on December 12, 1963, under Jomo Kenyatta. His son, Uhuru Kenyatta, served as president from 2013 to 2022.

## Geography

Kenya straddles the equator, with diverse landscapes: Indian Ocean beaches, the Great Rift Valley, savanna grasslands, and Mount Kenya (5,199 m), Africa's second-highest peak. The Maasai Mara is famous for the annual wildebeest migration. Lakes Victoria, Turkana, and Nakuru are significant water bodies.

## Economy

Kenya has East Africa's largest and most diversified economy. Key sectors include agriculture (tea, coffee, flowers), tourism, and services. Nairobi is a major regional hub for finance, technology, and international organizations. Mobile money (M-Pesa) revolutionized financial inclusion globally.

## Culture

Kenya has over 40 ethnic groups, including the Kikuyu, Luo, Luhya, and Maasai. The country has produced world-famous long-distance runners and a vibrant music scene including benga and genge styles. Safari tourism and Maasai culture are internationally recognized.
