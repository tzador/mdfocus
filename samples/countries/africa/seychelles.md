**Seychelles**, officially the **Republic of Seychelles**, is an archipelago nation in the Indian Ocean. Comprising 115 islands, it has the smallest population of any sovereign African state and is known for its stunning beaches and unique wildlife.

## History

The islands were uninhabited until French colonization in 1756. Britain took control in 1814 after the Napoleonic Wars. Seychelles gained independence on June 29, 1976. A one-party state existed until 1993, when multiparty democracy was restored.

## Geography

Seychelles has two main island types: granite islands (including MahÃ©, Praslin, and La Digue) with lush vegetation and coral atolls. The Aldabra Atoll is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and home to giant tortoises. The climate is tropical, outside the cyclone belt.

## Economy

Tourism dominates the economy, drawn by pristine beaches and luxury resorts. Fishing (especially tuna) is the second major industry. Seychelles has Africa's highest GDP per capita. The country is developing financial services and offshore business registration.

## Culture

Seychellois culture blends African, European, and Asian influences. Creole cuisine features fresh fish and tropical fruits. Traditional music includes moutya, reflecting African heritage. The coco de mer palm, endemic to Praslin, produces the world's largest seed.
