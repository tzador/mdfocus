## History

The region was inhabited by Afar and Issa Somali peoples for centuries. France established a colony called French Somaliland in 1888, later renamed the French Territory of the Afars and the Issas. Djibouti gained independence on June 27, 1977.

## Economy

Djibouti's economy is based on its strategic location and service sector, particularly its port facilities. The Port of Djibouti handles most of landlocked Ethiopia's trade. The country hosts military bases from France, the United States, China, Japan, and Italy, generating significant revenue. Limited natural resources restrict other economic development.

## Culture

Djiboutian culture blends Somali, Afar, and French influences. Traditional nomadic lifestyle remains important to many, though most now live in urban areas. Poetry and oral tradition are highly valued. The country has a unique cuisine combining African and Middle Eastern elements.
