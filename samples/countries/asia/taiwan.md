## History

Taiwan's indigenous peoples were joined by Chinese settlers and colonized by Dutch and Spanish. Qing China ruled until Japan colonized the island (1895-1945). After WWII, the ROC government retreated to Taiwan in 1949 after losing the Chinese Civil War.

Taiwan democratized in the 1990s after decades of martial law. Relations with China remain tense, with ongoing tensions over sovereignty.

## Geography

Taiwan is mostly mountainous, with the Central Mountain Range running north-south. The west coast has plains and most of the population. The climate is subtropical to tropical. Taiwan lies on the Pacific Ring of Fire.

## Economy

Taiwan has a highly developed economy, crucial for global technology supply chains. Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company (TSMC) produces most of the world's advanced chips. Electronics, machinery, and petrochemicals are key exports.

## Culture

Taiwanese culture blends Chinese traditions with Japanese influence and indigenous elements. Traditional arts, temple festivals, and night markets are distinctive. Bubble tea originated here. The country has a free press and vibrant civil society.
