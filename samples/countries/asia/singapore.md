## History

Singapore was founded as a British trading post by Stamford Raffles in 1819. It was occupied by Japan in WWII. After a brief federation with Malaysia, Singapore became independent in 1965.

Under Lee Kuan Yew, Singapore developed rapidly through export-oriented industrialization, strict governance, and anti-corruption measures.

## Geography

Singapore consists of the main island and 63 smaller islands. Land reclamation has increased territory by 25%. The country is highly urbanized with green spaces preserved. The tropical climate is hot and humid year-round.

## Economy

Singapore has one of the world's most open and corruption-free economies. Key sectors include financial services, electronics manufacturing, petrochemicals, and shipping. The port is among the world's busiest.

## Culture

Singapore's culture reflects its Chinese, Malay, Indian, and Western influences. "Singlish" (English creole) is widely spoken. Hawker centers serve diverse cuisine. The country balances strict laws with multicultural tolerance.
