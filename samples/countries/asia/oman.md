## History

Oman has ancient trading connections with East Africa, India, and beyond. The Omani Empire controlled Zanzibar and coastal East Africa. Isolation under Sultan Said bin Taimur ended when his son, Qaboos, seized power in 1970.

Sultan Qaboos modernized Oman during his 50-year reign until his death in 2020. His cousin Haitham bin Tariq succeeded him.

## Geography

Oman has diverse landscapes: the Hajar Mountains, the Empty Quarter desert, fjords in Musandam, and green Dhofar monsoon region. The country controls the strategically important Strait of Hormuz. The climate is hot and arid.

## Economy

Oman has significant oil and gas resources, though less than neighbors. Diversification into tourism, logistics (Duqm port), and manufacturing is ongoing. The country maintains conservative fiscal policies.

## Culture

Omani culture is Arab and Islamic, with distinctive traditions. Ibadi Islam predominates, promoting tolerance. Traditional crafts include silverwork and shipbuilding. Oman is known for frankincense production and hospitality.
