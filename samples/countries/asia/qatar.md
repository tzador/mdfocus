## History

Qatar was a pearling center before oil discovery. It was a British protectorate until independence in 1971. The Al Thani family has ruled since the 19th century.

Qatar has pursued an independent foreign policy, angering neighbors who imposed a blockade (2017-2021). The country has invested globally and hosted major events.

## Geography

Qatar is a flat, arid peninsula jutting into the Persian Gulf. The climate is hot and humid. The country has no rivers and relies on desalinated water. Land reclamation has extended the coastline.

## Economy

Natural gas (the world's third-largest reserves) and oil have made Qatar extremely wealthy. The country has diversified into finance, aviation (Qatar Airways), and sports. Sovereign wealth funds invest globally.

## Culture

Qatari culture is Arab and Islamic, with traditional customs maintained alongside modernization. The Museum of Islamic Art and Education City reflect cultural ambitions. Traditional crafts and falconry are valued.
