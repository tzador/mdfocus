## History

Syria has ancient history as part of Mesopotamian civilization. Damascus claims to be the world's oldest continuously inhabited city. The region was part of Ottoman, then French mandate, with independence in 1946.

The Assad family ruled from 1970 until 2024. The Syrian Civil War, beginning in 2011, killed over 500,000 and displaced millions. The Assad regime fell in December 2024.

## Geography

Syria has diverse terrain: Mediterranean coast, fertile plains, desert, and the Euphrates River valley. The Golan Heights are occupied by Israel. Much infrastructure has been destroyed by war.

## Economy

Syria's economy has collapsed due to war and sanctions. Before 2011, agriculture, oil, and tourism contributed to a developing economy. Reconstruction costs are estimated in the hundreds of billions.

## Culture

Syrian culture is Arab with ancient Christian and other minorities. Damascus and Aleppo had renowned souks, mosques, and cultural heritage, much damaged by war. Syrian cuisine and music are part of broader Levantine traditions.
