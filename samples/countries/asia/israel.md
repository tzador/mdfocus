## History

The region has ancient significance for Judaism, Christianity, and Islam. Jewish kingdoms existed in antiquity before Roman destruction and diaspora. Modern Zionism led to increased Jewish immigration to Ottoman and British Palestine.

Israel declared independence in 1948, leading to war with Arab neighbors. Multiple wars followed. The Israeli-Palestinian conflict remains unresolved, with disputes over borders, settlements, and Palestinian statehood.

## Geography

Israel has diverse geography: Mediterranean coast, the Negev Desert, the Jordan Rift Valley, and mountainous regions. The Dead Sea is the lowest point on Earth. The country is small but strategically located.

## Economy

Israel has a highly developed economy, known as the "Startup Nation" for its tech sector. Diamonds, pharmaceuticals, and technology are major exports. Agriculture uses advanced irrigation in arid regions. The economy has grown despite regional tensions.

## Culture

Israeli culture blends Jewish traditions from around the world with Middle Eastern and Mediterranean influences. Hebrew was revived as a spoken language. The country has a vibrant arts scene, cuisine influenced by many cultures, and intense political discourse.
