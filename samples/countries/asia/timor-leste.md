## History

Portugal colonized East Timor from the 16th century. After Portugal's withdrawal in 1975, Indonesia invaded and occupied the territory, causing massive casualties.

Following a 1999 UN-supervised referendum, pro-Indonesian militias caused destruction before international intervention. Independence came in 2002. The country has faced political instability but is stabilizing.

## Geography

Timor-Leste occupies eastern Timor island plus the Oecusse enclave. The terrain is mountainous, with Mount Ramelau as the highest point. The climate is tropical. Coral reefs and marine biodiversity are significant.

## Economy

Timor-Leste is a lower-middle-income country heavily dependent on oil and gas revenues. The Petroleum Fund saves resource revenues. Agriculture (coffee) and fishing are important. Economic diversification is a priority as oil reserves deplete.

## Culture

Timorese culture blends Portuguese, Indonesian, and indigenous elements. Catholicism was adopted during Portuguese rule and Indonesian occupation. Traditional animist beliefs persist. Tais woven cloth is a traditional craft.
