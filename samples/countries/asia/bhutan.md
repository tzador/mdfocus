**Bhutan**, officially the **Kingdom of Bhutan**, is a landlocked country in the Eastern Himalayas. Known as the "Land of the Thunder Dragon," it is famous for measuring Gross National Happiness and its preserved Buddhist culture.

## History

Bhutan has been an independent kingdom for centuries, never colonized. Buddhism was introduced in the 7th century and became central to national identity. The country was unified under Zhabdrung Ngawang Namgyal in the 17th century.

The monarchy transformed from absolute to constitutional in 2008. Bhutan remained isolated until the 1960s and still carefully manages tourism and development.

## Geography

Bhutan is entirely mountainous, ranging from subtropical plains to glaciated peaks over 7,000 meters. Over 70% of the country is forested. The terrain makes infrastructure development challenging. The country is the world's only carbon-negative nation.

## Economy

Bhutan has a small economy based on hydroelectric power exports to India, tourism, and agriculture. Tourism is high-value, low-volume, with minimum daily tariffs. Traditional architecture is required for all buildings. Gross National Happiness guides policy.

## Culture

Bhutanese culture is deeply Buddhist, with monasteries (dzongs) dominating the landscape. Traditional dress (gho and kira) is required for official occasions. Archery is the national sport. The country has strict environmental and cultural preservation policies.
