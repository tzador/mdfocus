## History

The Malacca Sultanate was a powerful trading state before Portuguese, Dutch, and British colonization. Malaysia gained independence in 1957, with Borneo states joining in 1963. Singapore was expelled in 1965.

Under Mahathir Mohamad's long leadership, Malaysia industrialized rapidly. Recent politics have seen anti-corruption movements and changes in government.

## Geography

Malaysia consists of two regions separated by the South China Sea: Peninsular Malaysia and East Malaysia (Borneo). The country has tropical rainforests, highlands, and extensive coastlines. Borneo hosts some of the world's oldest rainforests.

## Economy

Malaysia has a diversified upper-middle-income economy. Electronics, palm oil, and petroleum are major exports. Manufacturing and services drive growth. The country aims to achieve developed-nation status.

## Culture

Malaysian culture reflects its Malay, Chinese, Indian, and indigenous populations. Islam is the official religion, but religious diversity exists. Traditional arts, batik, and wayang kulit continue alongside modern pop culture. Food reflects multicultural influences.
