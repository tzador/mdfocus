## History

Japan's recorded history begins in the 8th century, with the Emperor as a symbolic figure. Feudal shoguns ruled for centuries. The Meiji Restoration (1868) rapidly modernized Japan. Militarism led to World War II, ending with atomic bombings and U.S. occupation.

Post-war Japan experienced an "economic miracle," becoming the world's second-largest economy. The country maintains a pacifist constitution and close alliance with the United States.

## Geography

Japan is an archipelago of nearly 7,000 islands, with four main islands: Honshu, Hokkaido, Kyushu, and Shikoku. Located on the Pacific Ring of Fire, it experiences frequent earthquakes and has many volcanoes, including Mount Fuji. The climate varies from subarctic to subtropical.

## Economy

Japan has a highly developed industrial economy, known for automobiles, electronics, and robotics. Companies like Toyota, Sony, and Honda are global leaders. The economy faces challenges from an aging population and slow growth.

## Culture

Japanese culture uniquely blends tradition and modernity. Shintoism and Buddhism coexist. Traditional arts include tea ceremony, ikebana, and kabuki theater. Anime, manga, and gaming have global cultural influence. Cuisine (sushi, ramen) is UNESCO-recognized.
