## History

The Maldives was a Buddhist kingdom before converting to Islam in the 12th century. It was a Portuguese, then Dutch, and finally British protectorate until independence in 1965.

The country was ruled by Maumoon Abdul Gayoom for 30 years until 2008 democratic reforms. Political instability and alleged corruption have characterized recent politics.

## Geography

The Maldives comprises about 1,200 coral islands in 26 atolls, with only 200 inhabited. No point rises more than 2.4 meters above sea level, making climate change an existential threat. The islands have pristine beaches and rich marine biodiversity.

## Economy

Tourism dominates the economy, with luxury resorts on private islands. Fishing is the traditional livelihood. The country imports most goods. Climate adaptation and protecting coral reefs are priorities.

## Culture

Maldivian culture is Islamic and South Asian-influenced. Traditional dhoni boats and lacquer work are distinctive. Bodu Beru drumming is a traditional art form. The country has strict Islamic laws, though resorts operate differently.
