**Brunei**, officially **Brunei Darussalam**, is a small, wealthy country on the island of Borneo. One of the world's last absolute monarchies, it is known for its oil wealth and strict Islamic laws.

## History

The Bruneian Empire was a powerful sultanate controlling much of Borneo and the Philippines in the 15th-16th centuries. British influence grew in the 19th century, establishing a protectorate in 1888. Independence came on January 1, 1984.

Sultan Hassanal Bolkiah has ruled since 1967. Sharia law was fully implemented in 2019, drawing international criticism.

## Geography

Brunei consists of two non-contiguous parts on the northern coast of Borneo, surrounded by the Malaysian state of Sarawak. The terrain is mostly lowland rainforest. The country has extensive oil and gas fields offshore.

## Economy

Brunei is one of the world's wealthiest countries per capita, thanks to oil and gas exports. Citizens enjoy no income taxes and subsidized housing, education, and healthcare. The government is working on economic diversification as oil reserves decline.

## Culture

Bruneian culture is Malay and Islamic. The sultan's palace, Istana Nurul Iman, is the world's largest residential palace. Traditional Malay customs coexist with strict Islamic observance. Alcohol is banned.
