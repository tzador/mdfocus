## History

The region has ancient history, including the Nabataean Kingdom (builders of Petra). It was part of Roman, Byzantine, and Ottoman empires. Britain created Transjordan after WWI, and independence came in 1946.

The Hashemite monarchy has provided stability. Jordan has absorbed waves of refugees from Palestine, Iraq, and Syria. The country maintains peace with Israel since 1994.

## Geography

Jordan is mostly arid desert, with the Jordan Rift Valley in the west. The Dead Sea is shared with Israel and Palestine. The country has limited water resources. Wadi Rum's desert landscape is famous for its beauty.

## Economy

Jordan has limited natural resources and a developing economy. Services, tourism, and phosphate mining are key sectors. The country depends on foreign aid and remittances. Refugee populations have strained resources.

## Culture

Jordanian culture is Arab and Islamic, with Bedouin traditions valued. Hospitality is paramount. The country has well-preserved archaeological sites including Petra, Jerash, and Crusader castles. Traditional food includes mansaf (national dish).
