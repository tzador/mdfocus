**Thailand**, officially the **Kingdom of Thailand** (formerly Siam), is a country in Southeast Asia. Known for its temples, beaches, and cuisine, it is the only Southeast Asian country never colonized by a European power.

## History

Thai kingdoms, including Sukhothai and Ayutthaya, ruled for centuries. Siam modernized under Chakri dynasty kings. The 1932 revolution ended absolute monarchy. Thailand allied with Japan in WWII but avoided colonization.

Modern Thailand has experienced political instability, with multiple coups. The monarchy remains revered. King Vajiralongkorn succeeded his father Bhumibol in 2016.

## Geography

Thailand has diverse landscapes: mountains in the north, the Khorat Plateau in the northeast, the fertile Central Plain, and tropical southern peninsulas with islands. The Chao Phraya River is the main waterway.

## Economy

Thailand has Southeast Asia's second-largest economy. Tourism, manufacturing (automobiles, electronics), and agriculture (rice, rubber) are key sectors. The Eastern Economic Corridor aims to attract high-tech industry.

## Culture

Thai culture centers on Buddhism, the monarchy, and hospitality. Temples (wats) are everywhere. Thai cuisine is globally popular. Traditional arts include classical dance, Muay Thai boxing, and elaborate crafts.
