## History

Mesopotamia, between the Tigris and Euphrates, saw the rise of Sumer, Babylon, and Assyriaâ€”among the world's first civilizations. Baghdad was the Abbasid Caliphate's glorious capital. Ottoman rule lasted until World War I, followed by British mandate.

Saddam Hussein's dictatorship (1979-2003) included the Iran-Iraq War and Gulf War. The 2003 U.S. invasion led to his overthrow but also sectarian violence and the rise of ISIS.

## Geography

Iraq is mostly desert, with the fertile river valleys of the Tigris and Euphrates in the center. The Kurdish region in the north is mountainous. The climate is hot and arid. The Shatt al-Arab waterway provides the only coastline.

## Economy

Iraq has one of the world's largest oil reserves, which dominate the economy. Reconstruction after decades of war continues. The economy suffers from corruption, infrastructure deficits, and political instability. The Kurdish region has autonomous economic governance.

## Culture

Iraqi culture reflects its Arab and Kurdish populations and ancient heritage. Baghdad was historically a center of learning. Iraqi music, literature, and cuisine are rich traditions. Ancient sites like Babylon and Ur are of global significance.
