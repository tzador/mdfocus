## History

The Kyrgyz people are descended from nomadic tribes. The region was conquered by Russia in the 19th century and became part of the Soviet Union. Independence came in 1991.

Kyrgyzstan has experienced political instability, including revolutions in 2005, 2010, and 2020. Despite turbulence, it maintains a more open political system than its neighbors.

## Geography

Kyrgyzstan is over 90% mountainous, with the Tian Shan range dominating. Issyk-Kul is one of the world's largest alpine lakes. The country has spectacular natural beauty but challenging terrain for infrastructure.

## Economy

Kyrgyzstan is one of Central Asia's poorest countries. Gold mining (Kumtor) is the largest industry. Agriculture, particularly livestock, is important. Remittances from migrant workers in Russia are crucial.

## Culture

Kyrgyz culture celebrates nomadic heritage, with yurts, horsemanship, and eagle hunting as traditions. The Epic of Manas, an epic poem, is a national treasure. Traditional felt crafts (shyrdaks) are recognized by UNESCO.
