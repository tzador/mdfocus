## History

Chinese civilization is one of the world's oldest, with dynasties ruling for over 4,000 years. The Republic of China replaced imperial rule in 1912. After civil war, the Communist Party established the PRC in 1949 under Mao Zedong.

Economic reforms under Deng Xiaoping from 1978 transformed China into a manufacturing powerhouse. Under Xi Jinping, China has become more assertive globally while maintaining strict domestic control.

## Geography

China spans diverse landscapes: the Himalayas and Tibetan Plateau, the Gobi Desert, vast plains, and extensive coastline. The Yangtze and Yellow Rivers are historically significant. The country has significant environmental challenges including air pollution and water scarcity.

## Economy

China has the world's second-largest economy (largest by PPP) and is the leading manufacturing nation. The country has lifted hundreds of millions from poverty. Technology, infrastructure, and global trade are key sectors. Economic growth has slowed from previous decades.

## Culture

Chinese culture has profoundly influenced East Asia and the world. Key elements include Confucianism, Chinese characters, traditional medicine, cuisine, and arts (calligraphy, opera, martial arts). Major holidays include Chinese New Year and Mid-Autumn Festival.
