## History

The Kingdom of Georgia flourished in the medieval period, with its Golden Age under Queen Tamar. After Mongol invasions and fragmentation, Georgia was absorbed into the Russian Empire in 1801. Soviet Georgia existed from 1921 to 1991.

Independence brought civil war and separatist conflicts. The 2008 war with Russia resulted in Russian occupation of Abkhazia and South Ossetia. Georgia has pursued European integration.

## Geography

Georgia is mountainous, with the Greater Caucasus in the north and Lesser Caucasus in the south. The Black Sea coast has a subtropical climate. The country has remarkable biodiversity. Tbilisi sits in a valley on the Mtkvari River.

## Economy

Georgia has a developing economy with tourism, agriculture (wine, hazelnuts), and transit routes as key sectors. Economic reforms have improved the business environment. The country attracts tourists to its mountains, Black Sea resorts, and wine regions.

## Culture

Georgian culture is ancient and distinctive, with its own unique alphabet and polyphonic singing (UNESCO recognized). The Georgian Orthodox Church is central to national identity. The country claims to be the birthplace of wine. Georgian cuisine, particularly khachapuri and khinkali, is renowned.
