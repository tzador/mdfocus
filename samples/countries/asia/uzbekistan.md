## History

The region was home to ancient civilizations and Silk Road trading centers. Timur (Tamerlane) built an empire from Samarkand. Russia conquered the area in the 19th century.

Soviet Uzbekistan existed until independence in 1991. Islam Karimov ruled autocratically until 2016. His successor, Shavkat Mirziyoyev, has implemented limited reforms.

## Geography

Uzbekistan has diverse terrain: fertile valleys, the Kyzylkum Desert, and mountains in the east. The Amu Darya River flows along the border. The Aral Sea crisis, caused by Soviet irrigation, is an environmental catastrophe.

## Economy

Uzbekistan has Central Asia's largest population and economy. Cotton (historically through forced labor), gold, uranium, and natural gas are key exports. Economic reforms are attracting investment. Tourism to historic cities is growing.

## Culture

Uzbek culture is rich in history and craftsmanship. Samarkand, Bukhara, and Khiva have stunning Islamic architecture. Traditional arts include ceramics, embroidery, and music. Uzbek pilaf is a national dish.
