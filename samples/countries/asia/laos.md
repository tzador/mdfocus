**Laos**, officially the **Lao People's Democratic Republic**, is a landlocked country in Southeast Asia. Known for its French colonial heritage and Buddhist temples, it is one of the world's few remaining communist states.

## History

The Lan Xang kingdom ruled from the 14th century. France colonized Laos in the 19th century. After WWII, civil war and the Vietnam War devastated the country. The communist Pathet Lao took power in 1975.

Laos is the most bombed country in history per capita, with unexploded ordnance still killing people. Economic reforms since the 1980s have opened the economy while maintaining one-party rule.

## Geography

Laos is mountainous and forested, with the Mekong River forming much of its western border. The country is landlocked, bordered by five countries. The Plain of Jars features mysterious ancient megaliths.

## Economy

Laos is a developing economy, with hydroelectric power exports, mining, and agriculture as key sectors. China has invested heavily in infrastructure, including a high-speed railway. Tourism is growing but limited by infrastructure.

## Culture

Lao culture is heavily influenced by Theravada Buddhism. Temples (wats) are central to community life. Traditional music and dance, the Baci ceremony, and sticky rice cuisine are distinctive. French colonial architecture remains in cities.
