## History

The Kazakh steppes were home to nomadic peoples for millennia. The Kazakh Khanate existed from the 15th century. Russian and then Soviet control lasted until independence in 1991.

Nursultan Nazarbayev led Kazakhstan for nearly 30 years until 2019. The country has pursued economic development while maintaining authoritarian governance. Astana was renamed after Nazarbayev briefly, then reverted.

## Geography

Kazakhstan stretches from the Caspian Sea to China. The terrain includes vast steppes, deserts, and mountains in the east. The country has significant environmental issues from Soviet-era nuclear testing and the shrinking Aral Sea.

## Economy

Kazakhstan has Central Asia's largest economy, driven by oil, gas, and minerals. The country has attracted significant foreign investment. Economic diversification beyond resources is a priority. The Baikonur Cosmodrome is leased to Russia for space launches.

## Culture

Kazakh culture reflects nomadic heritage, with horsemanship and hospitality central to tradition. Traditional music uses the dombra (two-stringed instrument). The country has a mix of Kazakh and Russian influences. Islam is the majority religion.
