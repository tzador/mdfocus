## History

Indonesian islands have been inhabited for over a million years (Java Man). Hindu-Buddhist kingdoms flourished, including Srivijaya and Majapahit. Islam spread from the 13th century. Dutch colonization lasted from the 17th century until independence in 1945.

Sukarno led the independence movement and early nation. Suharto's authoritarian "New Order" (1967-1998) was followed by democratic reforms. Indonesia is now Southeast Asia's largest economy.

## Geography

Indonesia stretches 5,000 km across the equator, from Sumatra to Papua. It lies on the Pacific Ring of Fire with over 130 active volcanoes. Major islands include Java (most populous), Sumatra, Borneo (Kalimantan), Sulawesi, and Papua.

## Economy

Indonesia has Southeast Asia's largest economy, with manufacturing, mining, agriculture, and services as key sectors. It is a major producer of palm oil, rubber, and nickel. The country is developing a new capital, Nusantara, in Borneo.

## Culture

Indonesian culture is diverse, with over 300 ethnic groups and 700 languages. Javanese and Balinese cultures are prominent. Batik textiles, gamelan music, and wayang shadow puppetry are UNESCO-recognized traditions. Islam coexists with Hindu, Buddhist, and animist elements.
