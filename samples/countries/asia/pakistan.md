## History

Pakistan was created from the partition of British India in 1947, an event that caused massive displacement and violence. The country lost East Pakistan (Bangladesh) in 1971.

Pakistan has oscillated between civilian and military rule. Tensions with India, particularly over Kashmir, continue. The country has faced terrorism and extremism challenges.

## Geography

Pakistan has diverse landscapes: the Karakoram and Hindu Kush mountains (including K2, the world's second-highest peak), the Indus River valley, the Thar Desert, and Arabian Sea coastline. The Indus River is the country's lifeblood.

## Economy

Pakistan is a developing economy with agriculture, textiles, and services as key sectors. The country faces challenges including energy shortages, debt, and political instability. Remittances from overseas workers are significant.

## Culture

Pakistani culture blends South Asian and Islamic traditions. Urdu literature and poetry are highly valued. Music ranges from classical to Sufi qawwali. Cricket is the national passion. Regional cultures (Punjabi, Sindhi, Pashtun, Baloch) are diverse.
