## History

Armenia has ancient roots, with the Kingdom of Armenia at its peak under Tigranes the Great. Christianity was adopted in 301 CE. The Armenian Genocide of 1915 killed 1.5 million under Ottoman rule.

Soviet Armenia existed from 1920 to 1991. Independent Armenia has faced challenges including the Nagorno-Karabakh conflict with Azerbaijan, which resulted in territorial losses in 2020 and 2023.

## Geography

Armenia is mountainous, with Lake Sevan being the largest lake in the Caucasus. Mount Ararat, the national symbol, lies just across the Turkish border. The country has a continental climate with significant seasonal variation.

## Economy

Armenia has a developing economy based on mining, information technology, and agriculture (brandy, wine). The diaspora provides significant remittances. The country has emerged as a tech hub in the region despite limited natural resources.

## Culture

Armenian culture is ancient and distinctive, with its own alphabet created in 405 CE. The Armenian Apostolic Church is central to national identity. Armenian cuisine, music (duduk), and literature are celebrated. Armenians have a large global diaspora.
