## History

Bahrain has been inhabited since ancient times, known as Dilmun in Mesopotamian texts. The Portuguese and Persians ruled before the Al Khalifa dynasty took control in 1783. Bahrain was a British protectorate until independence in 1971.

The Arab Spring sparked protests in 2011, primarily by the Shia majority against the Sunni ruling family. These were suppressed with Gulf Cooperation Council assistance.

## Geography

Bahrain is an archipelago of 50+ islands, mostly desert. The main island is connected to Saudi Arabia by the King Fahd Causeway. The climate is hot and humid. Land reclamation has significantly expanded the country's area.

## Economy

Bahrain pioneered oil production in the Gulf (1932) but has the region's smallest reserves. The economy has diversified into banking, tourism, and aluminum production. Bahrain hosts the Formula 1 Grand Prix. Financial services are a major sector.

## Culture

Bahraini culture reflects its trading heritage and Arab-Islamic traditions. The country is relatively liberal by Gulf standards. Traditional pearl diving, music, and crafts are celebrated. The population is mixed Sunni and Shia Muslim.
