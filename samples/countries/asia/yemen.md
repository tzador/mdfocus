## History

Yemen was home to ancient kingdoms including Sheba. It was important in the spice and coffee trade. North and South Yemen united in 1990 after different colonial and Cold War influences.

Civil war erupted in 2014 between the government and Houthi rebels. Saudi-led intervention began in 2015. The conflict has caused massive civilian casualties and famine.

## Geography

Yemen is mostly arid, with mountains in the west receiving more rainfall. The Rub' al Khali desert lies in the northeast. Socotra Island has unique biodiversity. The country controls the strategic Bab-el-Mandeb strait.

## Economy

Yemen was already the Arab world's poorest country before the war. Oil and gas were important, but infrastructure is destroyed. Agriculture, fishing, and remittances provided livelihoods. War has created famine conditions.

## Culture

Yemeni culture is Arab and Islamic, with distinctive architecture (tower houses of Sana'a). Qat chewing is a widespread social custom. The country has rich traditions in poetry, music, and crafts.
