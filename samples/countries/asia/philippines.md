## History

Pre-colonial Philippines had various settlements and trade connections. Spain colonized the islands for over 300 years (1565-1898). After the Spanish-American War, the U.S. controlled the Philippines until independence in 1946.

Ferdinand Marcos ruled dictatorially (1972-1986) until the People Power Revolution. Democratic governance has continued, with Marcos' son winning the presidency in 2022.

## Geography

The Philippines is an archipelago of 7,641 islands, with Luzon and Mindanao being the largest. The terrain is mostly mountainous, with extensive coastlines and coral reefs. The country lies in the typhoon belt and Ring of Fire.

## Economy

The Philippines has a growing economy based on services (BPO industry), remittances, manufacturing, and agriculture. English proficiency supports call center and tech industries. Overseas Filipino Workers support the economy significantly.

## Culture

Filipino culture uniquely blends Malay, Spanish, American, and Chinese influences. Catholicism (introduced by Spain) is dominant. Family values, hospitality, and fiestas are central. Music and entertainment industries are vibrant.
