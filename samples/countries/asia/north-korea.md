## History

Korea was divided after WWII, with the north under Soviet influence. Kim Il-sung established the DPRK in 1948. The Korean War (1950-1953) killed millions but ended in stalemate.

The Kim dynasty has ruled since 1948: Kim Il-sung, Kim Jong-il, and currently Kim Jong-un. The country developed nuclear weapons, leading to international sanctions.

## Geography

North Korea is mountainous, with plains in the west. The climate has cold winters and warm summers. The Demilitarized Zone (DMZ) separates it from South Korea. Natural resources include coal and minerals.

## Economy

North Korea has a command economy, isolated by sanctions and mismanagement. Famines in the 1990s killed hundreds of thousands. The country prioritizes military spending. Limited economic zones and trade with China provide some foreign exchange.

## Culture

North Korean culture is heavily controlled and centered on the Kim personality cult. Juche (self-reliance) ideology is promoted. Traditional Korean arts exist within state-controlled parameters. Mass games and monuments celebrate the regime.
