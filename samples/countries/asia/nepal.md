**Nepal**, officially the **Federal Democratic Republic of Nepal**, is a landlocked country in South Asia. Home to Mount Everest and the birthplace of Buddha, it lies between China and India.

## History

Nepal was unified by Prithvi Narayan Shah in the 18th century. The Shah dynasty ruled, later constrained by Rana prime ministers. Nepal was never colonized but influenced by Britain.

A Maoist insurgency (1996-2006) led to the abolition of the monarchy in 2008 and establishment of a federal republic. Political instability has characterized the transition period.

## Geography

Nepal spans from tropical lowlands (Terai) to the highest peaks on Earth. Eight of the world's fourteen 8,000-meter peaks, including Mount Everest, lie wholly or partly in Nepal. The country has remarkable biodiversity across altitude zones.

## Economy

Nepal is a developing economy based on agriculture, remittances, and tourism. Mountaineering and trekking attract visitors worldwide. Hydropower potential is vast but underdeveloped. Labor migration to Gulf countries and Malaysia is significant.

## Culture

Nepali culture blends Hindu and Buddhist traditions. Temples and stupas dot the landscape. The Gurkha warrior tradition is famous. Festivals like Dashain and Tihar are major celebrations. Traditional crafts include thangka paintings and metalwork.
