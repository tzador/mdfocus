## History

Kuwait was a trading port before oil discovery. It became a British protectorate in 1899 and independent in 1961. The Al Sabah dynasty has ruled for centuries.

Iraq's invasion in 1990 and the subsequent Gulf War (1991) liberated Kuwait but caused massive destruction. The country has rebuilt and diversified since.

## Geography

Kuwait is mostly flat desert with a short Persian Gulf coastline. The climate is extremely hot in summer. There are no permanent rivers or significant water resources. Several islands lie offshore.

## Economy

Kuwait has about 10% of global oil reserves, making it one of the world's wealthiest countries. Oil revenues fund a generous welfare state for citizens. Diversification efforts include finance and investment. The Kuwait Investment Authority is one of the world's oldest sovereign wealth funds.

## Culture

Kuwaiti culture is Arab and Islamic, with Bedouin traditions. The country is relatively liberal by Gulf standards, with an elected parliament. Traditional dhow boats and pearl diving are historical heritage. Modern Kuwait City has transformed dramatically since the 1990s.
