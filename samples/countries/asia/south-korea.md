## History

Korea was divided after WWII, with the south under U.S. influence. The Republic of Korea was established in 1948. The Korean War (1950-1953) caused massive destruction but ended in stalemate.

Rapid industrialization under authoritarian rule (1960s-1980s) created the "Miracle on the Han River." Democracy was achieved in 1987. South Korea is now a leading democracy.

## Geography

South Korea is mostly mountainous, with plains in the west. The Han River flows through Seoul. The climate has distinct seasons, with monsoon summers. The DMZ separates it from North Korea.

## Economy

South Korea has the world's 10th-largest economy. Chaebols (conglomerates) like Samsung, Hyundai, and LG are global leaders in electronics, automobiles, and shipbuilding. The country invests heavily in R&D and education.

## Culture

Korean culture has achieved global influence through the "Korean Wave" (Hallyu). K-pop, K-dramas, and Korean cinema (Parasite) are internationally popular. Traditional culture includes hanbok, kimchi, and Buddhist temples. Education is highly valued.
