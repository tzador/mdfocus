## History

Tajiks are a Persian-speaking people with ancient history in the region. The area was part of the Russian Empire and then the Soviet Union. Independence came in 1991, followed by a devastating civil war (1992-1997).

President Emomali Rahmon has ruled since 1994, maintaining authoritarian control.

## Geography

Tajikistan is over 90% mountainous, with the Pamir range (the "Roof of the World") dominating the east. Glaciers are an important water source. The climate varies dramatically with altitude. The country is prone to earthquakes.

## Economy

Tajikistan is Central Asia's poorest country. Remittances from migrant workers (mainly in Russia) are crucial. Aluminum production and cotton are key industries. Hydropower potential is significant. Drug trafficking from Afghanistan is a persistent problem.

## Culture

Tajik culture is Persian-influenced, distinct from Turkic neighbors. Poetry, especially Rudaki, is revered. Traditional music and dance are important. Zoroastrian traditions persist alongside Islam. Hospitality and tea culture are central.
