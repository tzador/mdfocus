**Azerbaijan**, officially the **Republic of Azerbaijan**, is a country at the boundary of Eastern Europe and Western Asia. Known as the "Land of Fire" for its natural gas seeps, it is an oil-rich nation on the Caspian Sea.

## History

The region has ancient history, with various empires controlling the territory. Azerbaijan was briefly independent (1918-1920) before Soviet rule. Independence came in 1991.

The Nagorno-Karabakh conflict with Armenia has defined post-independence history. Military victories in 2020 and 2023 restored Azerbaijani control over the disputed region. The Aliyev family has ruled since 1993.

## Geography

Azerbaijan lies on the Caspian Sea coast. The terrain includes the Caucasus Mountains in the north, lowlands, and semi-desert regions. The country has significant biodiversity and mud volcanoes. The Nakhchivan exclave is separated from the main territory by Armenia.

## Economy

Oil and gas dominate Azerbaijan's economy, with pipelines exporting to Europe. Baku has transformed into a modern city with architectural landmarks. The government is working on economic diversification, including tourism and agriculture.

## Culture

Azerbaijani culture blends Turkic, Persian, and Russian influences. Mugham music is a UNESCO-recognized tradition. The country is predominantly Shia Muslim but largely secular. Carpet weaving and hospitality are important cultural elements.
