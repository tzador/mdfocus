## History

Indigenous peoples inhabited the region. The Dutch, then British, colonized, bringing enslaved Africans and later indentured Indians. Independence came on May 26, 1966. The country was long associated with Forbes Burnham's authoritarian rule. Recent oil discoveries have transformed prospects.

## Geography

Guyana is mostly tropical rainforest. The coastal plain is agricultural and where most people live. Kaieteur Falls is among the world's most powerful waterfalls.

## Economy

Oil production (starting 2019) is transforming Guyana's economy, making it one of the world's fastest-growing. Traditional sectors include sugar, rice, and gold mining. Managing oil wealth responsibly is the key challenge.

## Culture

Guyanese culture reflects its diverse population: Afro-Guyanese, Indo-Guyanese, and indigenous peoples. Cricket is popular. Diwali and Carnival are both celebrated.
