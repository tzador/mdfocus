## History

The Mapuche people resisted conquest longer than other indigenous groups. Chile gained independence in 1818. The Pinochet dictatorship (1973-1990) followed a US-backed coup against Allende. Democracy was restored, and Chile has been stable since.

## Geography

Chile stretches 4,300 km from Peru to Cape Horn. It includes the Atacama (world's driest desert), the Andes, fjords, and Patagonia. Easter Island and Juan FernÃ¡ndez Islands are Chilean. The country is earthquake-prone.

## Economy

Chile has Latin America's highest GDP per capita. Copper mining dominates exports (Chile produces 25% of world copper). Wine, salmon, and lithium are important. The economy is relatively market-oriented.

## Culture

Chilean culture features distinct traditions: the cueca dance, poetry (Neruda, Mistral), and seafood-heavy cuisine. The country has produced two Nobel Prize-winning poets.
