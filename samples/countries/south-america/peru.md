## History

Peru was the center of the Inca Empire, the largest pre-Columbian American civilization. Spanish conquest (1532) and colonial rule were followed by independence in 1821. Internal conflict (Shining Path insurgency) caused significant violence in the 1980s-90s.

## Geography

Peru has three distinct regions: the coastal desert (including Lima), the Andean highlands (including Cusco and Machu Picchu), and the Amazon jungle. Lake Titicaca is shared with Bolivia.

## Economy

Peru is a major producer of copper, gold, and silver. Fishing and agriculture (including asparagus and superfoods like quinoa) are important. Tourism generates significant revenue.

## Culture

Peruvian culture blends indigenous and Spanish heritage. Peruvian cuisine (ceviche, causa) is internationally acclaimed. Festivals combine Catholic and Andean traditions. Traditional textiles and music remain vibrant.
