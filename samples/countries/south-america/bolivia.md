## History

## Geography

Bolivia spans the Andes (including the Altiplano plateau) to Amazon lowlands. La Paz is the world's highest capital at 3,640m. The Salar de Uyuni is the world's largest salt flat. Lake Titicaca is shared with Peru.

## Economy

Bolivia has significant natural gas and lithium reserves (the Salar de Uyuni contains large deposits). Mining and agriculture are important. The country is one of South America's poorest.

## Culture

Bolivian culture reflects its indigenous majority. Aymara and Quechua traditions are strong. Traditional festivals blend Catholic and indigenous elements. The pollera (traditional skirt) and bowler hats are distinctive.
