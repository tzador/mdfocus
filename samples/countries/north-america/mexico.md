## History

Mexico was home to advanced civilizations including Olmec, Maya, and Aztec. Spanish conquest (1519-1521) destroyed the Aztec Empire. Independence came in 1821. The 1910 Mexican Revolution shaped modern Mexico.

Mexico experienced long single-party rule (PRI) until 2000. NAFTA integration with the US and Canada transformed the economy.

## Geography

Mexico has diverse terrain: deserts in the north, tropical forests in the south, the Sierra Madre mountains, and extensive coastlines on both Pacific and Gulf. Volcanic and seismic activity is significant.

## Economy

Mexico has a large, diversified economy. Manufacturing (especially automotive), oil, tourism, and remittances are key sectors. Near-shoring is bringing new investment.

## Culture

Mexican culture is globally influential: food (tacos, mole), music (mariachi), art (Rivera, Kahlo), and traditions (Day of the Dead). Indigenous and Spanish heritage blend distinctively.
