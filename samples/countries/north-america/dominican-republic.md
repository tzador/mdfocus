## History

Hispaniola was Columbus's first permanent New World settlement. The colony changed hands between Spain and France. The Dominican Republic declared independence from Haiti in 1844. US occupation (1916-1924) and Trujillo's dictatorship (1930-1961) marked the 20th century.

## Geography

The Dominican Republic has the Caribbean's highest peak (Pico Duarte) and lowest point (Lake Enriquillo). Beaches, mountains, and tropical forests characterize the diverse terrain.

## Economy

Tourism is the leading industry, centered on beach resorts. Free trade zones produce textiles and electronics. Agriculture (sugar, tobacco, coffee) remains important.

## Culture

Dominican culture is vibrant, known for merengue and bachata music. Baseball produces many MLB players. Carnival and religious festivals are celebrated enthusiastically.
