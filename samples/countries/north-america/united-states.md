## History

Indigenous peoples inhabited North America for thousands of years. European colonization began in the 16th century. The 13 colonies declared independence from Britain in 1776. The Constitution established the federal government in 1789.

Expansion westward, the Civil War (1861-1865), industrialization, and two World Wars shaped America. The US emerged as a superpower after WWII and became the sole superpower after the Soviet collapse.

## Geography

The US spans from Atlantic to Pacific, including Alaska and Hawaii. Landscapes include mountains (Rockies, Appalachians), plains, deserts, and diverse coastlines. The Mississippi River system drains the interior.

## Economy

The US has the world's largest nominal GDP, leading in technology, finance, entertainment, and military spending. It is a major producer of oil, natural gas, and agricultural products.

## Culture

American culture is globally influential through Hollywood, music (jazz, rock, hip-hop), technology, and fast food. The "American Dream" and individualism are cultural themes. Diversity characterizes the population.
