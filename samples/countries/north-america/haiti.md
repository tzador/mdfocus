## History

The Haitian Revolution (1791-1804) created the world's first successful slave revolt and the first Black republic. Independence in 1804 was followed by isolation, occupation (US 1915-1934), and dictatorship (Duvaliers). Political instability, natural disasters, and poverty have persisted.

## Geography

Haiti is mountainous, with significant deforestation. The climate is tropical with hurricane risk. Environmental degradation compounds economic challenges.

## Economy

Haiti is the Western Hemisphere's poorest country. Agriculture, remittances, and light manufacturing are economic bases. Aid dependence is significant.

## Culture

Haitian culture is rich: Vodou religion, distinctive art and music (compas), Creole language, and the revolutionary heritage of Toussaint Louverture.
