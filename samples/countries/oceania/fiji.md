## History

Fiji was settled by Melanesian and Polynesian peoples. Britain colonized in 1874, bringing Indian indentured laborers. Independence came on October 10, 1970. Multiple coups (1987, 2000, 2006) have marked post-independence politics.

## Geography

Fiji comprises over 330 islands, about 110 inhabited. The two largest islands are Viti Levu and Vanua Levu. The islands are volcanic and coral. The climate is tropical.

## Economy

Tourism dominates, with resorts on outer islands. Sugar, garments, and gold are exports. Remittances from Fijians abroad are significant.

## Culture

Fijian culture is Melanesian-Polynesian, with yaqona (kava) ceremonies central to social life. Indo-Fijians maintain distinct traditions. Rugby is the national passion.
